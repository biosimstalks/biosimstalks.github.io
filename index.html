BioSim Talks

An IPR Seminar Series in collaboration with WPI-PRIMe

We are a research group at the University of Osaka (PRIMe) studying molecular mechanisms that shape the behavior of biomolecular systems in health and disease. We focus on how proteins and other biomolecules interact with small molecules, peptides, regulatory partners, and therapeutic compounds, and how mutations or drugs modify these interactions in ways that affect biological function.

Our work uses molecular dynamics simulations, including atomistic models, enhanced-sampling methods, free-energy calculations, and coarse-grained approaches, complemented by structural modeling and docking. We collaborate closely with experimental groups and computational partners to bridge molecular-level behavior with broader biological and clinically relevant questions.

Some of the systems we study include receptor tyrosine kinases and their signaling complexes, growth-factor receptor pathways, and viral proteins such as the SARS-CoV-2 Spike.

Welcome to the monthly BioSim Seminar Series. Here we host talks on biological simulations, computational biophysics, and systems biology.
